Restarts

How to restart play

Free Kick: Direct or Indirect
Penalty Kick: 12 yards from goal
Throw-in: From touchline
Goal Kick: From 6-yard box
Corner Kick: From corner arc
Dropped Ball: Where play was last stopped

Cards

When to show cards

Yellow Card (Cautionable Offences):
  • • Unsporting behaviour:
    • ◦ Reckless challenge
    • ◦ Throwing objects
    • ◦ Sarcastic gestures
    • ◦ Showing lack of respect for match officials or the game
  • • Dissent by word or action
  • • Persistent infringement of the Laws of the Game
  • • Delaying the restart of play
  • • Failing to respect required distance when play is restarted:
    • ◦ Corner kick
    • ◦ Free kick
    • ◦ Throw-in
  • • Entering, re-entering or deliberately leaving the field of play without the referee's permission
  • • Persistent unacceptable behaviour:
    • ◦ Repeated warning offences
    • ◦ Showing a lack of respect
Red Card (Sending-Off Offences):
  • • Serious foul play
  • • Violent conduct
  • • Spitting at an opponent or any other person
  • • Denying goal-scoring opportunity by an offence punishable by a free kick or penalty kick:
    • ◦ Including deliberate handball
  • • Using offensive, insulting or abusive language and/or gestures
  • • Receiving a second yellow card in the same match

Offside

Offside basics

Offside Position (not an offense by itself):

A player is in an offside position if:

  • • They are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent
  • • They are in the opponent's half of the field

➡️ A player is not offside if they are level with the second-last opponent or the ball.

Offside Offense (becomes an offense only if involved in active play):

A player in an offside position is penalized only if, at the moment the ball is played or touched by a teammate, they become involved in active play by:

  • • Interfering with play (playing or touching the ball passed or touched by a teammate), or
  • • Interfering with an opponent (preventing them from playing or being able to play the ball — e.g., obstructing their line of vision or challenging them for the ball), or
  • • Gaining an advantage (playing a ball that rebounds or is deflected to them off the goalpost, crossbar, match official, or an opponent having been in an offside position)
No Offside Offense When:

The player receives the ball directly from:

  • • A goal kick
  • • A throw-in
  • • A corner kick

Handball

Handball decisions

Handball Offense:
  • • Deliberate hand/arm contact
  • • Hand/arm above shoulder
  • • Hand/arm makes body bigger
  • • Hand/arm in unnatural position
  • • The goal scored/created with the hand of an attacker (always offense, even if accidental)
Not Handball:
  • • Ball hits hand/arm directly
  • • Hand/arm close to body
  • • Supporting body in fall
  • • The deflection clarification (not offense if it comes from close range or own body quickly)

Signals

Referee signals

Common Signals:
  • • Direct Free Kick:
    • → Referee points straight in the direction of the kick
    • (Signal is held briefly; no arm raised)
  • • Indirect Free Kick:
    • → Referee raises one arm straight up
    • (Arm remains raised until the ball is touched by another player or goes out of play)
  • • Goal:
    • → Referee points with one arm toward the center circle
  • • Corner Kick:
    • → Referee points with one arm toward the corner flag on the attacking side
  • • Goal Kick:
    • → Referee points toward the goal area (6-yard box) with an open hand
  • • Advantage:
    • → Referee extends both arms forward and calls "Play on!" or "Advantage!"
Other Useful Signals:
  • • Penalty Kick:
    • → Referee points directly to the penalty mark
  • • Throw-In:
    • → Referee points in the direction of attack along the touchline
